Main Service - 'What are the Characteristics of a Healthy Church Member? (Part 1)'

Various Scriptures

Pastor Mark Barrett

The church is a visible extension of the light and life of Christ in the world.

1. A Healthy Church Member is a Regenerate Church Member

  • American church is full of unbelievers and focuses on supposedly transcendental experiences.
  • 1 John 3:9-10

No one born of God makes a practice of sinning, for God's seed abides in him; and he cannot keep on sinning, because he has been born of God. By this it is evident who are the children of God, and who are the children of the devil: whoever does not practice righteousness is not of God, nor is the one who does not love his brother.

  • Observe: no grey area, one group or another, and they are easily seen by commission of righteousness and omission of unrighteousness.
  • Matthew 25:31-46
  • 1 Thessalonians 1:1 - The church is identified as being those who are in God and redeemed by Jesus.

2. A healthy church member pursue holiness.

  • Hebrews 12:14
  • Ephesians 4:20-24 - No true Christian can not claim to love God without pursuing holiness because our God is holy.
  • Martin Lloyd Jones: Holiness isn't something we do to become something we aren't; it's something we do because of something we are.

3. A healthy church member loves Biblical preaching and teaching.

  • a. They know that Biblical Preaching _ a heart hungry for the Word of God.
    • The life of a Christian is organized around God's Word.
    • A Christian has surrendered their life to the authority of the Word.
    • Do you love preaching? Do you surround yourself throughout each day with godly preaching of the Word?
    • The Church that loves sound preaching will look more and more like Christ.
    • Spurgeon: "Whatever subject I preach, I do not stop until I reach our Savior." (approx.)
    • Psalms 119:103-104
  • b. They know Biblical Preaching _ a heart that is _ on the will of God.
    • We want the philosophies of the world to scatter.
    • God speaks through His Word, not subjective experience.
    • Hebrews 1
  • c. They know Biblical Preaching cultivates a life of Gospel integrity and doctrinal purity
    • Subjective 'truth' is turning aside in the opposite direction to follow their own authority, not God's.
    • Titus 2:11-12
  • d. They know that receiving Biblical Preaching benefits the entire congregation and encourages a pastor in his faithfulness to preaching.
    • Philippians 2:1-2
    • "Nothing discourages a pastor faster than a congregation inattentive to the Word of God."
    • 3 John 4
    • i. Preaching unified us in four ways
      • Being of the same mind
      • Having the same love: loving each other equally
      • United in spirit: Selflessness, living harmoniously with other believers
      • Intent on one purpose: To think the same thing, unified in what they believe

4. A healthy church member is a humble follower of Jesus Christ

  • James 4:10
  • 1 Peter 5:5-7
    • Matthew 5:3
    • The only people going into Heaven are those who know they are spiritually bankrupt.
  • Humility is the understanding of one's own spiritual bankruptcy when one has seen the perfect holiness of Christ.
  • Luke 7:36-39

Romans 11 Survey

Romans 11

Has God rejected the people of Israel?

God's relationship with Israel is defined by the covenants he made with them (Abrahamic Covenant, Mosaic Covenant)

  • Abrahamic Covenant - Genesis 12,15

    No if-then statements: Unconditionally promised.

    Facets promised: Land, Seed, and Blessing (to the whole world)

    • The Jews are still in the world today, millennia later, because of God's covenant with them.
    • Deuteronomy 7:6-8

      God chose a people, without regard to works or merit, for no reason that is apparent and clear to us.

    • The role of Israel was to produce the Messiah through whom blessing would be extended to all mankind.
    • The covenants to Israel should be considered true and real promises regarding the future state of Israel.

      Some presume the promises are forfeit to Israel as they crucified and reject their Messiah, and that their covenant has passed to us. (Covenant Theology vs. Dispensationalism)

      Mostly comes into play when talking about eschatology.

      • Covenant Theology's guiding principles
        • Covenant of Works
        • Covenant of Grace

        Heavy emphasis on continuity, so God does not have to change over time. This extends so far that the covenants must be perceived / chosen in such a way they apply to those it is scoped to eternally.

        This means they have to create discontinuity with mankind after Christ, the covenant withdrawing from Israel and being given to the Church, with all the condemnations of wrath being turned to Israel instead. This can be taken to a dangerous, antisemitic conclusion.

        In order to arrive at this conclusion, you'd have to 'spiritualize' (not interpret literally) a great deal of the Old Testament, reinterpreting OT passages based on NT passages, in ways the original audience would not have understood.

        Consequences:

        • The land of Israel is not important, and the eschatological reign of Christ on earth is meaningless, with no millennial reign.
        • No rapture required, no future in which Israel will witness the second coming and be saved.

        (Personal note: Hebrews 1 somewhat speaks in terms of dispensationalism?)

      • Dispensationalism guiding principles
        • Unique Administration in different Ages
        • One Path to Salvation

        Administration: Ways in which God works with His people - both with individuals and with peoples.

        Genesis 15 - Abraham's trust in God was credited to him as righteousness, despite Christ being 2000 years away.

        • Takes the literal meaning of covenant promises in the OT.
        • Keeps a separation between Israel and the Church
          • Matthew 16: Jesus refers to it as something new, a global organism, with Jews and Gentiles mingled, not a covenant constrained to one people. This is the future.
          • The Church is a 'mystery', not revealed to OT saints
          • Israel and the Church coexist together, especially in Acts when we see them treated as separate entities.
          • 1 Corinthians 10:32
          • In Romans 11, we see a remnant of Messianic Jews God is preserving

        God's warnings to Israel will continue to be enacted, but they will undergo a time when they will go through great distress, being purged and prepared for the second coming of their Messiah - this occurring during the Great Tribulation. God's redemptive plan brings them front and center in the last days.

Examining Romans 11

  • 1 God has not rejected his people.

    If they are rejected for their works, grace has no power.

  • Jeremiah 31 - God will take away Israel's sins; all Israel will be saved.
  • 33-36 - All this causes Paul to break out in worship.

Main Service - Psalm 119:25-32 - 'Encouragement for the Overwhelmed'

Pastor Rocky Wyatt

1. The Psalmist's Desperation

  • 25

    'cleaves', absolute adherence, clinging tightly

    • 2 Samuel 23:10
    • Naaman's leprosy clinging
    • Job 19:20

    In response, he calls on God to revive him. A recurring theme in Psalm 119:

    • Psalm 119:37,40,50,88,93,107,149,154,156,159
    • Isaiah 57:15
    • 2 Corinthians 1:8-10;12:9

    Always, the revival is in order to be able to be what God wants him to be, to follow God's Word, God's precepts.

  • 26

    The psalmist recounts the events of his life, how good God has been.

    • Philippians 4:6-8
    • Psalm 116:1-2
    • Hebrews 4:13-16

    In the midst of his desperation, he asks God to teach him, not for escape, not for deliverance.

  • 27

    'the way of your precepts' - how to walk in His ways, not just what His ways are. He wants strength to be what God has asked him to be, not for his own sake or desires.

    • vv. 34, 73

    Meditating on God's wondrous works is the means by which we learn the wisdom to walk His ways, to further the glory of God as extensively as He can.

  • 28

    The psalmist here feels like they are dissolving in tears. We should not turn inwards in response, or away from God. Heaviness of heart drains life away. Spurgeon would rather have grief than hardness of impenitence.

    The psalmist here needs God to sustain him, as he is absolutely unable to deal with the reality of life.

    • Isaiah 40:29
    • Ephesians 4:13-15
    • Philippians 4:13

2. The Psalmist's Direction

It's easy for the arrogant mind to question the sovereignty of God. Either you don't believe He's sovereign, or you're mad because He is.

  • 29

    The false way is already in the Psalmist. He is prone to wander, and indeed that is the mind's natural state.

    • Psalm 119:128
    • Jeremiah 17:5-10

    By God's grace, he is given the law of God instead, showing where to walk rightly.

  • 30

    As things are difficult, the psalmist's reaction is to first choose the faithful way. Rocky observes it's very easy to move from Biblical grief to personal selfishness.

    The Psalmist put God's rules before Him, acknowledging they are always right and he needs to confom to those rules.

3. The Psalmist's Determination

A trial can become all-absorbing, consuming all the thoughts of a person's mind and turning them towards themselves.

  • 31

    Despite what you feel like, you must decide what to think on.

    • Deuteronomy 11:32;13:4
    • Psalm 63:8

    He asks that God not put him to shame, which will not happen as God is faithful to His word.

  • 32

    The psalmist doesn't just walk in God's ways: he determines to run, putting all his energy and mind towards it. He regains his 'spiritual second wind', here.

    'Make my heart broad', 'enlarge my heart' - the enlargement of His disciples' mind and will; and freeing them to serve Him.

    "Don't waste your pain."

Application

The Word of God is essential to maintaining a biblical course in life.

  1. Choose the right way, in spite of how you may feel.
  2. Cling to God's testimonies.
  3. Run in God's commandments.

Don't underestimate the power of God in your life.

Faith Builders

The Life of David

Terry Tyler

A victorious psalm of God's triumph

  • Historical events
    • God exalted at Mt. Sinai
    • God with His people in the wilderness
    • God allowing them to conquer the land and the Jebusites
    • God allowing them to ascend Mt. Zion (Ark of the Covenant)
  • Present elements and future elements

    Things God has promised now and in the future (millennial reign)

  • A liturgical choir song
  • 1-18 God's triumphant ascent and rule from Sinai to Zion
    • General references
      • 1-3 Declare that God brings destruction on His enemies and gladness to His people.

        David is quoting Moses as they left Mt. Sinai, what they said every time God's cloud would move and the Ark set out.

        • Numbers 10:33-35

        'God, arise!' - God taking action.

        Enemies of God - those who fall short of loving Him.

        The image in v. 2 shows the utter powerlessness of God's enemies before His divine justice.

        This is to lead the people to rejoice.

      • 4-6 Sing praise to God for His kindness to the needy

        The people are commanded to sing a song of praise.

        'him who ride through the deserts' may capture a couple of ideas:

        • God's power traversing terrain easily that is perilous for man.
        • God's care for His people in those difficult places.

        Care for all, highlighting the weakest

        • Fatherless, those without care, guidance, provision
        • Widows, those without a protector, defenseless, vulnerable. God is their judge, bringing fairness and protection.
        • The 'solitary', those with no place of refuge: not just homeless, but isolated, alone.
        • Prisoners, hearts changed to bring them into prosperity (not necessarily wealth!)

        Notice compassionate and gracious are often coupled when describing God's character.

        • Exodus 34:6; 2 Chronicles 30:9; Nehemiah 9:17,31; Psalm 103:8;111:4;112:4;116:5; Joel 2:13; Jonah 4:2

        The rebellious are left without that blessing, parched.

    • Historical references
      • 7-10 Remember God's majestic presence and provision for His people from Sinai through the wilderness to the promised land
      • 11-14 Announce and celebrate God's victories over Gentile kings and the spoil for His people

        1 Samuel 18:6-7 Women carrying news from place to place in that culture, as seen in v. 11 here.

        13 When you lie down to rest, you are covered in wonderful splendor, God allowing His abundant provision even in rest.

        14 Zalmon seems to be a mountain, perhaps a dark volcanic mountain; Zalmon means 'dark one'. It could have been a rare event for snow to fall on it, generating excitement. Or like snow on a dark mountain, the scattered weapons may look like snow on the ground.

      • 15-18 Proclaim the majesty ad honor of God amid His heavenly hosts and dwelling place in Zion

        Like describing the palace and army of the Lord.

        18 Ascending to Mt. Zion

        Paul quotes this verse specifically: Ephesians 4:8-11

  • 19-35 God's continued and ultimate triumph and honor from all people
    • Present and future references
      • 19-23 Reflect on God's present and future protection of His people from His enemies

        22-23 from high and low, all the enemies of God will be brought to judgment and utterly humiliated.

      • 24-28a Celebrate the Lord's procession and presence in His sanctuary in Zion

        The ark is coming into Jerusalem, and the people are called to celebrate.

        2 Samuel 6, 1 Chronicles 15-16

      • 28b-31 Offer petition for God to continue His conquests and receive proper honor among the Gentiles at Jerusalem

        30 'beast that dwells among the reeds' - Likely pharaoh of Egypt, maybe the image of a crocodile

        The gentile enemies of God represented here as beasts

        29,31 This judgment would lead to some reaching out and turning to God; in the future, all nations will. Isaiah 2:2-5, Philippians 2:9-11

      • 32-35 Sing praise every nation to God and ascribe to Him strength, majesty, and power

        34 - 'in the skies' - in full display before all nations, presented through the nation of Israel. The people should be overwhelmed with gratefulness for that privilege.

  • Closing reflections
    • God is highly to be praised and worshipped

      This psalm recounts His faithfulness and works, his holiness, his majesty, his sovereignty, his provision, his generosity, his deliverance, in past, present, and future.

    • When God is seated in His rightful place, He is rightly exalted, and His people receive abundant care and blessing

      A right relationship with God and His people bestows blessing, and He will be rightly exalted in the earth.

      Does God occupy His rightful place in the sanctuary of your heart?

    • David was deeply passionate and motivated to properly exalt the Lord

      Is your heart also?

      Stay focused on the purpose of worship.

Evening Service - Psalm 32 - 'The Blessing of Forgiveness'

Pastor Brandon Ragsdale

Forgiveness is required by all, and given freely and totally to every believer, but we still live this life with an unredeemed flesh.

We are confronted with a regular need for continual forgiveness, as we wrestle with our infatuation with and immersion in our own sin.

Maskil - Didactic Psalm, teaching

Written by David, possibly after his great sin with Bathsheba and Uriah.

Three truths concerning God's forgiveness

  • 1. 1,2,11 God's forgiveness brings true happiness

    A deep-rooted, genuine happiness found in God alone. Satisfaction and contentment, derived from a right relationship with God. Relief and rest.

    • Because our sin has been covered, completely removed

      Symbolized clearly in the sacrificial system

      The guilt has been lifted away

      Psalm 103:11-12

    • Because of the imputation of our sin onto Christ, and the imputation of Christ's righteousness onto us.
    • Because you are truly penitent.

      2 'no deceit'; no secret sin, their life is an open book. No excuses or justifications.

      Deceit here: scheming and plotting. Like cancer, if sin is not dealt with quickly, it will infect and destroy. Live above reproach, make your life a glass house.

  • 2. 3-7 God's forgiveness requires genuine repentance
    • A. 3-4 The rejection of repentance

      May have been literally wasting away, but more likely David is describing intense spiritual agony and anguish. Wanting to do anything to remove the feeling of guilt but remove the sin into the light of day and deal with it.

      David says God's hand was heavy upon him, loving discipline - only given to believers. God must discipline those He loves. This degree of agony is not experienced by those lost in sin here on earth.

      Hebrews 12:4-11 This discipline is required, necessary for God's justice and our sanctification.

    • B. 5-6 The result of repentance

      This is a public confession by David, matching the extent of his sin, observing forgiveness was given. He uncovered, and God removed it.

      Colossians 3 Continual conviction of sin by the Spirit that we must mortify.

      Proverbs 13:15

    • C. 7 The refuge for the repentant

      One who is right with God is sheltered by Him.

  • 3. 8-11 God's forgiveness motivates ongoing obedience

    The speaker here shifts to God Himself in 8-9.

    God pushes the believer towards obedience so as not to impose upon God's grace, towards repentance of sin.

    God's eye is on the believer - you cannot hide deeds or heart from God. He knows whether you are truly repentant.

    Image of a brute animal that doesn't learn, is stubborn, and must be kept in check by force and pain, broken by its handler.

    The wicked experience the natural consequences of their own sin, but those who are no fools, who trust in the Lord, living in the confidence of God, experience His love.

    This leads David at last to rejoice abundantly for that forgiveness. He is rejoicing even in the consequences that still came from that sin, because his source of joy and contentment was still in God and forgiveness of sins.

[2019-02-15 Fri 06:30] Session 1 - Dr. Tedd Tripp - Getting to the Heart of Behavior

Man is both corporeal and spiritual - when Scripture refers to the heart, it refers to the core of us, our thinking, our being.

Major teaching in the OT

  • 1 Samuel 16:7 God looks at the heart, not the outward appearance
  • Deuteronomy 10:12 God wants our whole hearts
  • 1 Chronicles 28:9
  • Proverbs 3:5-6
  • 2 Chronicles 16:9
  • 1 Kings 8:57-58

Heart directs behaviour

  • Matthew 5:21-30 The actions of the heart alone condemn

Out of the heart overflows behaviour

  • Matthew 15:17-20 The Pharisees focus on physical behaviour, but Jesus points out that the discharge of the heart is what makes a man unclean. The list here in v. 19 is vividly displayed in the behaviour of children, transparently.
  • Mark 7:20-23 More extensive list here
  • Luke 6:43-45 - We want a tree that bears good fruit, not hanging fruit on a bad tree by trying to manipulate that external behaviour:
    • bribing ('here's a reward if you behave well')
    • mechanical coercion ('tell your sister you're sorry')
    • shaming ('this is why we don't do anything fun')
    • heaping guilt ('it makes me so sad when I see you acting like this')

Those fruits will rot without the life-giving source of a good tree.

Proverbs 4:23

Ungodly hearts overflow in an ungodly attitude, leading to ungodly behaviour.

Godly hearts overflow in a godly attitude, leading to godly behaviour.

It is easy to try and treat the obvious, external behaviour, but our evaluation and treatment must run deeper. Evil is very good at translating external manifestations while preserving the internal attitude.

Hooking greed, selfishness, malice, pride to our motivations for our children's behaviour is even more damaging, only feeding the corrupt roots of that behaviour: doing the right thing for the wrong behaviour, 'whitewashed tomb' - clean on the outside, thoroughly rotten and decaying on the inside.

If we deal with the heart, we stand in solidarity with them as someone who also struggles with that same sin, instead of hypocritical separating yourself from your child as someone who is holier-than-thou, and demonstrate repentance and faith: the ultimate goal.

Behaviorism Evaluated

  1. Real need is not addressed
  2. False basis for ethics
  3. The heart is wrongly trained
  4. The gospel will not be central
  5. Shows our idols of the heart: control, pride, ease, convenience, fear of man

Describe admonishment in terms of the heart - 'kind and unkind', not just 'don't do that'

  • Pride and performance
  • Pleasure and sensuality
  • Possessions
  • Fear of man
  • Desire to be approved

Anything that - when absent - removes any joy found in God; or anything that is the most vital thing in your life, and in which God is minimized or even absent as the giver of every good gift.

  • Solid and lasting joys are found in knowing God. We so often take joy in our kids' joy in stuff that will not satisfy. We feed our children's idols!
  • You must be dazzled by God to dazzle your kids.

[2019-02-16 Sat 09:00] Session 3 - Philip De Courcy - The Prodigal Father

1 Samuel 2:12-36 A bad lesson learned from Eli and his sons.

The author writes with a degree of embarrassment.

The Word of the Lord hadn't shown for some time. The sons of the High Priest had turned the sanctuary of God into a brothel. Eli abdicated his own authority, failing to restrain his own sons. This is a spiritual autopsy of a deadbeat dad.

1. Contempt

  • 17 These two young men showed contempt for the things of God.

    A witlessness concerning the things of God. Contrast with Hannah's prayer in vv. 1-10.

    The very priesthood here is profaning the holy.

  • 12 Worthless men

    2 Corinthians 6:13 'Belial', that which is demonic in colloquial Hebrew

  • 13-17 Taking what they want, by force if necessary, in clear violation of God's Word.
  • 22 They turned the sanctuary into a brothel
  • 24 They led the people of God into sin
  • 29 God sees the contempt for His gifts being thrown back in His face.
  • How can they be in such flagrant sin? Where is their fear of God? How could they impinge on the patience of God? Familiarity breeded contempt.
