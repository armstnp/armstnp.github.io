Psalm 68 - 'The God of Sinai and of the Sanctuary'

Teacher
Terry Tyler

A victorious psalm of God's triumph

Historical events

  • God exalted at Mt. Sinai
  • God with His people in the wilderness
  • God allowing them to conquer the land and the Jebusites
  • God allowing them to ascend Mt. Zion (Ark of the Covenant)

Present elements and future elements

Things God has promised now and in the future (millennial reign)

A liturgical choir song

1-18 God's triumphant ascent and rule from Sinai to Zion

  • General references
    • 1-3 Declare that God brings destruction on His enemies and gladness to His people.

      David is quoting Moses as they left Mt. Sinai, what they said every time God's cloud would move and the Ark set out.

      • Numbers 10:33-35

      'God, arise!' - God taking action.

      Enemies of God - those who fall short of loving Him.

      The image in v. 2 shows the utter powerlessness of God's enemies before His divine justice.

      This is to lead the people to rejoice.

    • 4-6 Sing praise to God for His kindness to the needy

      The people are commanded to sing a song of praise.

      'him who ride through the deserts' may capture a couple of ideas:

      • God's power traversing terrain easily that is perilous for man.
      • God's care for His people in those difficult places.

      Care for all, highlighting the weakest

      • Fatherless, those without care, guidance, provision
      • Widows, those without a protector, defenseless, vulnerable. God is their judge, bringing fairness and protection.
      • The 'solitary', those with no place of refuge: not just homeless, but isolated, alone.
      • Prisoners, hearts changed to bring them into prosperity (not necessarily wealth!)

      Notice compassionate and gracious are often coupled when describing God's character.

      • Exodus 34:6; 2 Chronicles 30:9; Nehemiah 9:17,31; Psalm 103:8;111:4;112:4;116:5; Joel 2:13; Jonah 4:2

      The rebellious are left without that blessing, parched.

  • Historical references
    • 7-10 Remember God's majestic presence and provision for His people from Sinai through the wilderness to the promised land
    • 11-14 Announce and celebrate God's victories over Gentile kings and the spoil for His people

      1 Samuel 18:6-7 Women carrying news from place to place in that culture, as seen in v. 11 here.

      13 When you lie down to rest, you are covered in wonderful splendor, God allowing His abundant provision even in rest.

      14 Zalmon seems to be a mountain, perhaps a dark volcanic mountain; Zalmon means 'dark one'. It could have been a rare event for snow to fall on it, generating excitement. Or like snow on a dark mountain, the scattered weapons may look like snow on the ground.

    • 15-18 Proclaim the majesty ad honor of God amid His heavenly hosts and dwelling place in Zion

      Like describing the palace and army of the Lord.

      18 Ascending to Mt. Zion

      Paul quotes this verse specifically: Ephesians 4:8-11

19-35 God's continued and ultimate triumph and honor from all people

  • Present and future references
    • 19-23 Reflect on God's present and future protection of His people from His enemies

      22-23 from high and low, all the enemies of God will be brought to judgment and utterly humiliated.

    • 24-28a Celebrate the Lord's procession and presence in His sanctuary in Zion

      The ark is coming into Jerusalem, and the people are called to celebrate.

      2 Samuel 6, 1 Chronicles 15-16

    • 28b-31 Offer petition for God to continue His conquests and receive proper honor among the Gentiles at Jerusalem

      30 'beast that dwells among the reeds' - Likely pharaoh of Egypt, maybe the image of a crocodile

      The gentile enemies of God represented here as beasts

      29,31 This judgment would lead to some reaching out and turning to God; in the future, all nations will. Isaiah 2:2-5, Philippians 2:9-11

    • 32-35 Sing praise every nation to God and ascribe to Him strength, majesty, and power

      34 - 'in the skies' - in full display before all nations, presented through the nation of Israel. The people should be overwhelmed with gratefulness for that privilege.

Closing reflections

  • God is highly to be praised and worshipped

    This psalm recounts His faithfulness and works, his holiness, his majesty, his sovereignty, his provision, his generosity, his deliverance, in past, present, and future.

  • When God is seated in His rightful place, He is rightly exalted, and His people receive abundant care and blessing

    A right relationship with God and His people bestows blessing, and He will be rightly exalted in the earth.

    Does God occupy His rightful place in the sanctuary of your heart?

  • David was deeply passionate and motivated to properly exalt the Lord

    Is your heart also?

    Stay focused on the purpose of worship.

David seeks to restore true worship of God to Jerusalem by returning the Ark

Historical Narrative - Ark moved to Jerusalem

God moving into His chosen city

Teaches us something about the nature and character of God

Provides a positive example of how a Godly man should respond to the Lord and His lessons

The Dangers of Irreverence

  • 1 David called the leaders of Israel together.

  • 2a David and the people travel to Kiriath-Jearim to retrieve the ark.

    • Called by the name of God, Yahweh, emphasizing that it was so closely connected to God and God's presence among His people, you could not separate the two. 'Lord of Hosts' - Lord of armies, the heavenly hosts; His command over His created creatures. Demanding highest reverence.
    • God's throne on earth. His throne over His people, Israel. God was Israel's king.
  • Lessons from the Ark
    • God is the ruler of His people
    • God is holy (see: Cherubim, the highest order of God's created beings, with the purpose of protecting the holiness of God)

        • Inaccessible: resting in the Most Holy Place behind a curtain; covered when transported.
    • God is righteous and just and demands judgment of sin
    • Humanity is thoroughly sinful
      • God could not permit sinful man to even approach, much less touch the ark, teaching His people about His nature.
    • God is merciful
    • God has revealed Himself
      • Also called 'The Ark of the Testimony', containing reminders of God's covenant with His people

    • Irreverence - a failure to appropriately respect the holiness of God
      • Uzzah's view of God was not high enough, and he put his own thoughts above God's. Isaiah 55:8-9

    Isaiah 8:13

  • Abandoned his initial plan

  • David was reminded that the presence of God brings blessing.

    • He ordered that the ark be properly carried by the Levites

    • Walking by the word brings security rather than uncertainty

Strength

Steadfastness
reliability, endurance, permanence
Love
willing affection and generosity that seeks the benefit of the recipient
