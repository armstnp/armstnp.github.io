Two realities

  • God is Righteous, and only receives those who are righteous
  • None in Humanity are Righteous

Two ways to deal with those realities

  • Establish your own righteousness before your God
    • Isaiah 64:6 - In God's sight, all our righteous deeds are a filthy garment
  • Receive righteousness from God as a free gift through faith in Jesus Christ

Primary factors why people do not believe the Gospel

    • A. 30 Many Gentiles Have Gained Righteousness Apart from God's Law (mosaic law)
    • B. 31-33 No Jews Have Gained Righteousness Through the Law
      • 1. 31 The Fact
      • 2. 32-33 The Reasons
        • a. 32a They Stumbled Over the Means

          See also 10:3

        • b. 32b-33 They Stumbled Over the Messiah
    • The Messiah is "the Stone"

      • Isaiah 8:13-15; 28:16 (Paul directly quotes these in v. 33)

    • Isaiah 28:16 Christ, the cornerstone

      Crucial role: massive, squared within a fraction of an inch of perfection, then laid on carefully prepared ground. All other stones in the foundation were squared and trued to that cornerstone, and the rest of the building off of that foundation.

      • 3. There are only two possible responses to Jesus.

      • 4. True faith in Jesus Christ means He has become the cornerstone of your life!
      • 5. No one who believes in Jesus will be disappointed, dishonored, or shamed at the judgment!

        lit. 'out of the law'

        This is true even in earthly kingdoms - a violation of a single law is not compensated by keeping other laws. Even a claim to keeping God's law is only self-deception, as His standard is perfect, and none can meet it.

        Deuteronomy 27:26, Galatians 3:10 - 'You are under a curse if you do not abide by all things in the book of the law.' James 2:10-11 - We cannot cherry-pick the law. Romans 3:19-20; 8:7-8 - 'cannot', capacity; incapable

          Paul points that Christ already has done the impossible; these kinds of impossible demands aren't made of us.

        • 8 What this righteousness does say

          It's so accessible, it requires only your mouth and heart. Christ has done all required.

      5:8-9, 3:21-

      • 1. 9 Believe in your heart that God raised Jesus from the dead

        'believe that' - True saving faith has content; you can only believe in what you know.

        The confession: 'Jesus is Lord', meaning:

        • Jesus is the one, true and living God; greek 'curios' is used for God's personal name in Septuagint
        • Jesus is master - John 13:12-16 - We must submit to Jesus, as He is greater and commands our obedience.

        This isn't separate from believing, but still subsequent to it - you can only confess what you believe. Similarly, truly believing these things about Jesus in your heart will compel you to confess Him with your mouth.

        John 20:26-29 - Thomas' confession

      • If you are saved, there is no condemnation.
      • If you are unsaved, Paul makes it clear this applies to all.

11:1-32 The Reality of God's Faithfulness

Repeated structures in 1 and 11 mark two different sections.

I. 1-10 Israel's Current Spiritual Condition

  • A. 1-6 A Remnant Is Saved By Grace
    • 1. 1a The Question: Has God Permanently Rejected Israel?
    • 2. 1b,2a The Answer: Absolutely Not!
    • Application

      End of chapter 8: Paul argues we are absolutely secure in Christ.

      This is Paul's argument even to Gentiles that Israel is not a counterargument to our own salvation, but rather the proof of its security.

    • 3. 1c-4 The Proof
      • 1c Personal: Paul's own conversion to Christ

        Benjamin - the only son of Jacob born in the Promised Land; Jerusalem resided in its territory.

        Judah - the tribe of David - and Benjamin were both in the faithful south of Israel after its schism.

        Paul doesn't seem to make his point out of personal desire, but rather pointing out that as a Jewish Christian, he is proof that God has not wholly rejected His people.

        Indeed, Paul was the prime exemplar of this, given who he was before he was saved by God. 1 Timothy 1:13-16

      • 2a Biblical: God's OT promises not to forsake His people

        The wording of the initial question and his response here come directly from the OT: 1 Samuel 12:19-22 Psalm 94:14 Jeremiah 31:35-37

        Paul is refuting the idea that God has violated His promise to Israel.

        Central to God's nature is His inability to lie, to act contrary to His own nature: Numbers 23:19 Titus 1:2 Hebrews 6:18

        The ethnic descendants of Israel are still the special objects of God's care.

        There is therefore no place for anti-semitism in the heart of the believer, both because they are made in the image of God, and because of this special care He holds.

        We can also trust God will keep His promises to you. He is never undependable; He has never lied, never failed, and never will. He is incapable of doing so.

      • 2a Theological: God's electing love of the nation

        'To-know before' προέγνω, from προγινώσκω

        This must be referring to the entire nation of Israel:

        • It best fits the immediate context from 1
        • It best fits the entire context of chapter 11
        • Election of the nation is in view Romans 11:28

        Amos 3:2

        9:4-5 Through Israel, He brought the Messiah

        Deuteronomy 7:7-8;10:14-15;32:8-9

        This does not imply the current nation of Israel is filled with believers, nor does it mean God will save every member of Israel (as we have seen in prior chapters). But the special purpose of Israel as a nation does not guarantee individual salvation.

        Human sin cannot cancel out the promises of God. 3:3-4

        8:29 Just as He foreknew Israel, He foreknows those He saves, and just as He never will reject Israel, He will never reject you, believer.

      • 2b-4 Biblical: God's response to Elijah

        9:27 introduces the idea of a 'remnant' from Isaiah.

        In OT it refers to a spiritual remnant who are saved; embodies both judgment and hope.

        In chapter 9, 'remnant' is used negatively; here, it is used positively.

        Paul makes clear here that the idea is not a new one.

        'appeals' or 'pleads' - to intercede on behalf of someone. 8:27,34 But here, it's used in a negative way, against Israel.

        After Solomon's death, his son Rehoboam was foolish and the kingdom was divided; Judah and Benjamin in the south ('Judah'), and the other ten tribes in the north ('Israel').

        Jeroboam created their own custom religion in the north as a political ploy to prevent people from having to travel to Judah to worship. One place of worship was in Dan and Bethel, on the north and south ends of the kingdom. Gold images of calves are placed at these worship centers; these weren't images of God Himself, he said, but just things you could worship God through, or that He stood on, etc. This syncretist ploy tried to marry pagan Ba'al worship to worship of the true God, and He was condemned as it became a regular practice in the land.

        Over time, the worship refocused to worship Ba'al, and within 50 years after Solomon, under the reign of King Ahab son of Omri, the evil grew exceedingly great.

        1 Kings 16:25-19:14

        'Ethbaal' - 'Ba'al is alive', a priest of Ba'al, who murdered his predecessor.

        'Asherah' - the consort of Ba'al

        • 3a An Accurate Assessment

          Elijah's diagnosis of the state of Israel is almost entirely accurate: his spiritual diagnosis of the worship of Ba'al was accurate.

          'demolished' - razed, total intolerance of worship of Yahweh.

        • 3b A Mistaken Conclusion

          Elijah magnified the pain of his circumstances and minimized God's power, bringing him to depression and willingness to forsake his own life.

        • 4 God's Surprising Correction

          'a divine oracle'

          • He has always had a remnant.

            'kept' - to preserve in existence; God kept His people by His own action. Paul, in the guidance of the Spirit here, adds 'for Myself' to clarify God's intent.

            This clearly parallels in what Paul is saying now.

          • The remnant is always larger than we think.

            God preserved '7,000 men'; but this is specifically the word 'males', so even more than that had been saved.

          • The true believing remnant doesn't bow to Ba'al.

            Recall what Ba'al worship entailed, what you expected to receive from it: sexual pleasure and materialism.

        • The Lessons
          • 1. God has not forsaken Israel.
          • 2. God is saving more Jewish people than we think.
          • 3. God always has a larger remnant than we think.
          • 4. The spiritual majority are never right.

            Scope: The world at large.

            Therefore, remain confident in the Scripture; numbers do not matter, only truth does.

          • 5. God most often accomplishes His purposes in quiet and seemingly insignificant ways, rather than dramatic ones.

            While He can, He often doesn't, and we shouldn't take the absence of observable signs as an indication that He is not still working.

            Christ's church will be built.

    • 4. 5-6 The Implications
      • a) 5a There still exists a believing remnant.

        There has been no change in Israel's spiritual condition.

        The expression here notes the remnant has come into existence, unexpectedly. Acts 2:41;4:4;6:1a

        Acts 21:20 uses 'myriads', 'tens of thousands'

        Even out of the world at large, all believers make a remnant out of the full population.

      • b) 5b That remnant exists only because of God's choice.

        The remnant was not selected for their own works, but by God's gracious choice.

        Again, 'I have kept for myself…' in 4 and 'according to the choice of grace' in 5.

        'choice' - election, seen and discussed earlier in 11

      • c) 5b God's choice of the remnant is by grace alone.

        'grace' - not examining the kindness of the act, but the fact that the choice was totally of God. The attribute of God that delights in giving people good, the exact opposite of what they deserve.

      • d) 6 God's grace toward the remnant is completely apart from works.

        Paul reiterates this same point he's made repeatedly. Why insert it here?

        Tom's considerations:

        • Paul was overwhelmed with this doctrine, and consistently returns to it, as he loved it personally.
        • Paul knew how difficult it was for us merit-based people to accept grace without our own works added to it.

        Spurgeon: The human heart is so naturally opposed to grace, even if we know we have no part in our salvation, we still want some part in the last mile.

        He makes the point that grace and works are mutually exclusive as means for salvation.

        'no longer' - does not imply that there used to be a different means, but rather that the logical argument can no longer be maintained for works as a means.

        God's choice excludes anything a human does, good or evil; it is impossible for man to in any way influence or affect God's choice, lest it not be God's choice at all, and therefore no longer be grace.

        To bring his primary point back home, God has not abandoned His people, and nothing they have done can or will subvert His free giving of grace to the remnant He selected.

    • Implications
      • 1. Every believer in Jesus Chris is part of the believing remnant.

        Consider the remnant of the Noahic judgment. He would have been just not to preserve a remnant at all.

      • 2. The only reason you are part of that remnant is God's choice of you.

        Are you personally amazed at the sheer magnitude of God's grace in saving you?

      • 3. God's choice of you - and therefore your salvation - is not according to your works but by grace alone.
        • If any of your hope or confidence of being right with God or of getting into heaven is because of your own works, you are not a Christian.

          Galatians - 'another gospel'

          Faith and confidence must solely be in Jesus Christ. Romans 3:24-25

        • If you are a Christian, verse 6 refutes the idea that God chose you based on His foreknowledge of your faith.
        • Since God didn't choose you because of your works but His grace, it's His grace and not your efforts that guarantee your final salvation.
  • B. 7-10 The Rest Are Being Hardened by Sin

    Paul identifies three groups.

    • 1. 7a Israel as a Whole Has Not Obtained Righteousness.

      'seeking' - root word for seek, but Paul uses an intensifying prefix: intense search. Also in present tense, so this is an intense, ongoing, earnest search.

      The same language is used here as in 9:30-10:1,3-4

      Israel seeks to gain a right standing before God, but most failed to obtain it because thy were relying on their own works. Only about 16% of the Jewish people have professed faith in their Messiah.

      There is a danger in pursuing the right goal in the wrong way, and this same effect can be seen in churches today, as well.

      3:24-25;8:1,3 This is our salvation: Christ alone, our propitiation.

    • 2. 7b The Elect in Israel Have Obtained Righteousness

      9:27;10:16 Only a remnant

      9:11-12,15-16,18,29 Sovereign election is the cause

      'but the election obtained it' - Paul does not call them 'the elect', to avoid the idea that the elect were elected by inherent merit.

      1 Thessalonians 1:4

      Paul brings us back to the grace of God again.

    • 3. 7c-10 The Rest of Israel Were Hardened
      • a. 7c Stated & Defined

        'hardened' - in secular Greek, used to define medical conditions, like calcification of a gall stone or around a healing broken bone.

        2 Corinthians 3:14 A hardened mind, difficulty comprehending the truth.

        Here, Paul describes a hardened heart, as well; a stubborn, resistant heart.

        'were hardened' - passive tense; in context, the actor is God Himself. Romans 9:18;11:8

        Hardening is a judicial act of God, based on the rebellion or unbelief of the sinner. This is a judgment we all deserve.

        Note that God does not actively produce evil, sin, or unbelief in the heart. He does not tempt any man. Instead, He simply passes them by and withholds the softening effects of His Spirit, allowing them to have the results of what they want: their sin. It permanently binds people in the sin they have chosen for themselves.

        The sinner begins by hardening his own heart against God, and it ends in God judicially hardening the sinner's heart.

        Compare to the relationship between the sun and Texas clay. The sun hardens the clay, not directly, but rather by acting on inherent properties of the clay itself (see in contrast what the sun does to wax). Similarly, God allows the inherent properties of the sinner to take hold.

        Compare also to an imprisoned criminal; this often makes them not repentant, but a hardened criminal. The jail doesn't harden them, but it reinforces the existing properties.

        Hebrews 3:13 If you do not respond to the Word, the more you hear it, the harder your heart becomes. Hardening your heart to the truth is deadly serious.

        Nehemiah 9:16-17,26-31 This is a mark of Israel's history.

        Ephesians 4:17-18 The same happens for every unbeliever.

      • b. 8-10 Defended

        He chooses three passages: from the Law, the Prophets, and the (poetic writing?)

        • Isaiah 29:10

          'stupor' - total lack of spiritual sensitivity, as one blind drunk; self-induced

        • Deuteronomy 29:4
        • Psalm 69:22-23

          An imprecatory Psalm; not vengeful, but rather a call for God's justice on those who will not turn to Him out of a concern for God's glory and God's people. Here it represents a righteous man being attacked by his enemies, and Paul uses it to point to Christ and His eventual vindication by God.

          The spiritual advantages given to them would become their downfall instead.

          'retribution' - repayment, usually for punishment that a sin deserves. This is divine justice enacted, not capricious. Lamentations 3:64-66

    • Lessons from this passage
      • 1. 33-34 A lesson in submitting to God's Wisdom.

        Do not argue or question God or throw His word aside to come up with your own plan.

        Let God be God. Wrestle with it, but don't compromise a high view of God in the process.

      • 2. A lesson in anticipating God's justice.
        • If you are not a believer in Jesus Christ, this is a sobering reminder that God is perfect in His justice - toward you - and a call to repentance and faith. The longer you wait to submit, the harder it will become. I plead for you in sorrow to accept God's grace now, today.
        • If you are a believer and your heart is often heavy over all the injustice in the world, this passage should encourage you that one day justice will come.

II. 11-32 Israel's Future Salvation

Four Basic Propositions

  1. 11-12 As a whole, Israel has rejected her Messiah and His gospel.
  2. 11-12a Israel's rejection of Jesus Christ has resulted in the salvation of the Gentiles.

3a. 14 The salvation of the Gentiles is currently leading to the salvation of a few from Israel… 3b. 15,25-26 …but will eventually lead to the ultimate salvation of all Israel.

  1. 12b The ultimate salvation of all Israel will produce even greater blessing for the world.

Paul is addressing a common point of pride among Gentiles over the people of Israel, boasting that we have replaced the people of God; instead, he points out we've been grafted in.

  • A. 11-16 The Certainty of Her Future Salvation
    • 1. 11a An Obvious Question - Has Israel Permanently Fallen?

      A question of Israel's corporate status.

      Romans 9:31-33;10:3 gives us the context for this fall.

      Isaiah 24:20 Same word fall, showing the magnitude of the devastation of the fall being described.

    • 2. 11b An Emphatic Denial - Never!

      Deuteronomy 4:31;9:27 1 Samuel 12:22 Isaiah 43:21

    • 3. 11c The Divine Reasons
      • 9:22-23 To Display His Glory

        His wrath, power, patience, mercy, and grace are all put on display by this action.

      • Romans 11:11c To Bring Salvation to the Gentiles

        Transgression: singular, Israel's collective sin of unbelief in their Messiah. 20,23

        This was always part of God's plan, to use the Jewish people to redeem the world.

        "And in you, all the nations of the Earth will be blessed."

        Isaiah 49:4-7 Acts 13:45-47;18:4,6;19:8-9;28:28

      • 11c To Bring Salvation to the Jewish Remnant

        14 Moving them to jealousy; he wants them to see Gentiles enjoying God's blessing, and in turn to lead them to Christ.

    • 4. 12-15 God's Future Plans
      • 'Great Is Your Faithfulness'
        • 1. He is always consistent to act with His character.
        • 2. He is always reliable to speak the truth.
        • 3. He is always trustworthy to keep His promises.
        • 4. He is always loyal to His relationships.
      • 12-14 Israel Will Become a Greater Blessing to the Gentiles

        'the world', kosmos (Gr. κόσμος) - the unconverted Gentile world

        Another argument from the lesser to the greater: if in their failure the Gentiles were saved, what will the fullness of Israel be when they, too, are saved?

        'fullness' - brought to completion

        At the time of Israel's redemption, nearly - if not all - the people of Israel who are alive at that time will be saved.

        This book is addressed to Romans, gentiles; he wants us to understand the riches that will become ours when God restores Israel. Christ's millennial kingdom, and our reign with Him.

        Mark 14:24-25

        Paul was called as an apostle to the Gentiles. 1:1,5; 15:15-16,18 Acts 9:15

        Paul's humility: 'my ministry' (Gr. διακονία) - same word we get 'deacon' from: those who serve tables; lowly service of any kind.

        Paul's yearning is still to see His people saved. This is not his only or primary reason to serve, but it is a strong reason, one undiminished by their past failure.

        Paul desires that His ministry focused on the Gentiles would have a side effect in leading Israel to Christ.

      • 15 All Israel Will Receive Spiritual Life
    • 5. 16 Her Unique Position - holy or set apart to God

      Two illustrations

      Dough - 'the firstfruits' Numbers 15:17-21 By offering the firstfruits, you are acknowledging the full amount belongs to God as well.

      Olive Tree - 17 expands on this illustration, so he is clearly speaking of an olive tree. In Jeremiah 2:21 Israel is pictured as a grape vine; in Jeremiah 11:15-16, as an olive tree, so this makes sense as an illustration. If the root of the tree is God's, what grows out of the tree is God's.

      The second item in each illustration refers to the descendants of Abraham who have not yet been saved. The first item in each illustration refers to the portion that have been saved.

      Some believe these firstfruits are the first Jewish people to believe. But Tom tends to believe that's not the focus here; instead, it refers to the patriarchs. If they were set apart to God, their descendants were as well.

      Deuteronomy 10:14-15 is a key passage undergirding this reasoning. God will not abandon their descendants, because they are still set apart.

      28-29 re-emphasizes this point. God will not abandon His people.

  • B. 17-24 A Warning in light of Her Future Salvation

    Something in the Greek text not obvious in translation: it has a second person singular. This is addressed to each individual believer who is Gentile by birth.

    Olive trees were valuable primarily for their wood and their oil at the time. Oil was used for lamp fuel, cooking, medicinal purposes, and as a moisturizer.

    Two keys to understanding:

    The first key: the groups involved.

    • The olive tree itself is the true, believing people of God.
    • The root of the tree is the patriarchs and promises made to them.
    • The original branches that remain on the tree are the physical descendants of Abraham who believe.
    • The broken branches are the physical descendants of Abraham who do not believe.
    • The wild branches grafted on are Gentiles who believe.

    The second key: the main command - 18 'Do not be arrogant toward the branches' and 20 'do not become conceited'.

    'arrogant' - to boast at the expense of another; to revel in your defeat of or triumph over another.

    • 1. 17-18 Remember your real relationship to the people of God.
      • 17a You were not part of the tree.

        Wild olive trees were very bad at producing olives.

        9:4-5 Ephesians 2:11-13 The wild tree of the Gentiles was not connected to the cultivated tree, the promises given to the people of Israel.

      • 17-18a You were sovereignly grafted in and became a partaker of the root.

        7,20 The broken branches were cut off for their unbelief, and by God's hardening.

        What is the temptation to be arrogant? See: Douglas Moo

      • 18b You don't support the root, but the root supports you.

        Arrogance shows disdain for the Hebrew heritage of our faith.

        John 4:21-22 The true Jewish faith is our root.

        4:16-17 Galatians 3:6-9,14,29 There has always been one true people of God, and now that you've been grafted in, it doesn't matter which tree you came from. This tree has a distinctly Jewish root and heritage.

    • 2. 19-22 Remember your position is solely by faith, which means entirely by grace.

      Jewish unbelievers were broken off because they didn't believe

      Gentile believers were grafted for their faith

      Paul removes all cause for boasting, because we already have seen in Romans that faith is only available by grace.

      4:16; 9:32; 11:6

      This is something you did not work to earn.

      We should instead have fear

      Not because true believers can lose their salvation, by which he would contradict his repeated statements on security

      Rather, because true believers will always persevere in their faith until death or Christ's return.

      'The one who endures to the end shall be saved.' It's the outworking of faith, not the cause.

      "Perseverance is the hallmark of God's authentic children." (Quote from ?)

      Colossians 1:22-23 Hebrews 3:6

      These warnings are also for potential unbelievers who clearly do not bear fruit.

      "If you are not regularly following Christ in faithful discipleship, you are not His disciple.

      If you are not His disciple, you are not a Christian."

      'severity' - only usage of this Greek word in the Scripture

      Acts 13:43

      Ultimate salvation is dependent on continuing faith. Therefore the person who ceases to believe forfeits any hope of salvation. They prove they never really believed at all. 1 John 2:19

    • 3. 23-24 Remember God is able to add believing Jews back to the people of God.

      This is an unusual practice for grafting, to say the least, but that's the point.

      God can bring broken, fruitless branches back to life again.

    • Lessons
      • 1. 22 Don't allow a sentimental view of God to cloud your spiritual perspective.

        Kindness from God in this life does not mean you will escape His severity in judgment.

      • 2. John 15:1-2,5,8 Be warned that only those who manifest the fruit of righteous lives belong to the true people of God.
      • 3. Philippians 2:12 Persevere in your faith.

        1 Peter 1:5

      • 4. 17-18 Don't be arrogant toward the Jews - they still occupy the central place in God's redemptive plan and we [gentiles] have been graciously added.
      • 5. 23-24 Don't give up on those in your life who don't believe - God is able to give them faith and graft them in.
