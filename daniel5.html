Daniel 5 'When Empires Fall'

Overview

The message communicated in Daniel is God's absolute sovereignty, over even political systems and entire empires.

This chapter's theme is that God is absolutely sovereign over the rise and fall of empires and nations.

The events of this chapter occur around 70 years into the Babylonian exile.

Isaiah and Jeremiah both predicted repeatedly the judgment of Babylon.

Isaiah 46 - Written in the early 700s BC, where Daniel occurs in 539 BC

  • Babylon's idols had to be carried, but Israel's God carried them
  • God will fulfill His every sovereign will
  • 11 - The specific herald of Babylon's demise

Isaiah 47 - The Woe of Babylon

I. 1-4 The Defiance of YHWH: No government, no matter how defiant, is beyond God's sovereignty.

Belshazzar - 'Bel protect the king'

Bel being one of their gods

37+ texts dating from the first through fourteenth years of his reign verify his existence.

The Four Final Kings of Babylon

  • Nebuchadnezzar

    Reigned 43 years

  • Amel-Marduk

    Nebuchadnezzar's son, reigned two years Murdered by his brother in law, Neriglissar

  • Neriglissar
  • Labashi-Marduk

    Neriglissar's young son, became king in 556 BC;

  • Nabonidus

    Co-regent with his son, Belshazzar Lived elsewhere, never visited Babylon for fourteen years Wanted to worship the moon god instead of Marduk, so spent his time in Arabia where that worship was more prominent, appointing his son

    Belshazzar likely led the assassination of his father. Grossly immoral, compounded by blasphemy in this text.

Belshazzar held a great feast with a thousand nobles.

Oct 12 539BC Nabonidus chronicle says just two day before, king Cyrus of Persia marched towards the capital, routing the army badly and defeating his father. The day before, the city was under siege.

Usually would dine separate from their guests, but on holidays he'd eat with them. This is a show of bravado, drinking himself under the table.

Sine sort of festival that fell annually on this day.

Two purposes:

  • show calmness and normalcy to his people
  • show public contempt for the army outside the walls

Babylon had a massive 84-feet thick wall, 50-75 feet high, with 25 foot guard towers at regular intervals; then a thinner, higher, inner wall. Euphrates river provided moat and water source alike. Palace has an additional six walls, no less than 20ft. in height. 20 years of supplies stockpiled.

Belshazzar arrogantly considered his city impregnable.

This may have been in celebration of his formal coronation.

'tasted the wine' - becoming drunkenly influenced by the wine

The treasures had likely come from his grandfather (in Aramaic, father can be any prior generation)

These were taken from the temple in Jerusalem, as evidence their gods were more powerful than Israel's. This was PR for his own power. He knew YHWH had humbled Nebuchadnezzar, and this was a declaration YHWH would not similarly humble him.

Daniel 8:1-4,15-17,20

This was deliberate, challenging YHWH's prophecy.

II. 5-16 The Writing on the Wall: No empire, no matter how powerful or protected, is beyond God's reach.

A. 5-9 A man's fingers

The most well lit location in the room. Not a vision, as the writing remained for others to see. Purely miraculous.

Belshazzar seems to immediately understand the implication of this event. All dignity and decorum is gone, and he is in a state of sheer panic. His offered reward is reasonable given the position of his kingdom, and given he just received a message from a deity.

As always, the wise men prove wholly useless.

Why could they not read Aramaic?

  • Older script?
  • Old Phoenician?
  • Vertically instead of horizontally?
  • Only consonants in Aramaic

God shows their religious props to be utterly useless.

B. 10-12 The Queen Mother

Her timing is God's providence at work.

Either Belshazzar's grandmother or Belshazzar's mother (Nebuchadnezzar's wife or daughter)

Seems she knew Daniel personally, used his Hebrew name, respected him greatly, and may even have come to faith in the true and living God.

Daniel likely around 80, probably semi-retired since Nebuchadnezzar had died and a new regime had replaced the civil leaders.

C. 13-16 The King's Charge

The stage is set for Daniel.

Remember the theme of the passage: there are no impregnable rulers. Every ruler lives within the reach of the hand of Almighty God.

Luke 1:52 Isaiah 47:5-11, over 150 years earlier

III. 17-28 The Words of the Prophet: No ruler, no matter how wicked, is beyond God's verdict.

A. 17 Daniel's Introduction

He makes clear there is not even the appearance that God's gifts are not for sale. Daniel has a captive audience, here, of the entire royal court.

B. 18-21 Nebuchadnezzar's Story

The only true and living God had given Nebuchadnezzar his power. This power was utterly unrestrained and absolute; this in contrast to Belshazzar, a mere co-regent. God made an ass out of Nebuchadnezzar, literally, humbling him in his pride until he recognized God as the Lord of Lords.

C. 22-24 Belshazzar's Sin

  • 1. 22 Willful Disobedience

    Belshazzar likely saw these events first-hand; he knew what he was doing. Having the right information does not mean you will guarantee the right response.

  • 2. 23a Deliberate Defiance of the True God

    Daniel makes clear the utter banality of the idols of Babylon.

  • 3. 23b Arrogant Disrespect of God

    Despite knowing the power and reality of the LORD, he chose to worship his idols.

D. 25-28 God's Verdict

  • 'mene' - Your kingdom has been numbered by God, and its number is completed ('your number's up')
  • 'tekel' - Weighing the kingdom against the divine standard didn't even move the scale

    1 Samuel 2:3 This is what God does with every person. Proverbs 21:2;24:12 - God knows and will pay each according to his work

  • 'peres' - Singular form of 'upharsin' seen earlier. 'divided' - destroyed, dissolved

    Also wordplay, as 'PRS' constitutes the base of 'peres' and 'Persia'

IV. 29-31 The Revolution of History: No empire, no matter how improbable, rises or falls apart from God's decree.

A. 29 Daniel Rewarded

Possible he's keeping his word. Also possible he's trying to rope Daniel into the judgment. His response is unlike Nebuchadnezzar; he clearly believes the interpretation by this response towards Daniel. Proverbs 29:1

B. 30 Babylon Falls

Greek historians, in the decades and centuries following, give us details:

  • They chose this night deliberately, the night of a drunken feast
  • They dug a channel north, diverting the Euphrates into the swamp
  • The army waded through the shallow river
  • The bronze gates to the interior city were open, possibly treasonous action
  • This is prophecied clearly 150 years before in Isaiah 44:24-45:7.
  • The man who led Cyrus to Belshazzar was a man whose son Belshazzar had murdered in cold blood.

During all this, God was on His throne, accomplishing His exact purpose, overturning the Babylonian Empire in one cataclysmic day.

Additional Implications

2. It should shape your view of politics - local, national, and international.

3. It should remind all who are not in Christ of the inevitability of divine judgment.

Hebrews 9:27

4. It should direct your response to God: humble yourself, recognize God's sovereignty over your life breath and your ways and your total dependence on Him.

5. It should encourage you never to put your ultimate hopes in any earthly nation or kingdom.

Daniel 2:44 Hebrews 12:28
